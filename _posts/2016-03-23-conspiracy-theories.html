MK-Ultra

Project MK-Ultra was the codename of a secret CIA project that started around 1950. It aimed at developing methods for mentally controlling individuals using psychotropic substances and bioelectrical signals. Rumors about MK-Ultra circulated for more than 20 years until 1975 when the project was revealed to the public thanks to a report by the New York Times and congressional and presidential investigations that followed.

Asbestos

Everybody knows today that Asbestos exposure is a direct cause of serious and often fatal health issues like mesothelioma, asbestosis and lung cance. But during the 1920s and 1930s construction companies who were heavily using the material considered the health hazards of Asbestos merely a conspiracy theory against their interests. Years later the effects of Asbestos on human health were proven and most countries around the world have banned the use of the mineral.

Tuskegee syphilis experiment

Between 1932 and 1972, the U.S. government run a nontherapeutic experiment on a group of 399 black men from Macon County, Alabama. These men were infected with syphilis and were being used as Laboratory Animals: the government had no intention of curing them from syphilis, instead they were intentionally left without treatment to study the effect of syphilis on black men as opposed to white men. When the study was finally revealed to the public in 1972, news reporter Harry Reasoner referred to it as a study that "used human beings as laboratory animals in a long and inefficient study of how long it takes syphilis to kill someone."

Iran-Contra scandal

The Contras were Nicaraguan insurgents waging a guerrilla war against the Sandinista communist government backed by the Cubans. The conspiracy theory was that President Reagan facilitated the sale of US weapons to Iran to get them to help release American hostages from Iranian terrorists in Lebanon. And the proceeds from the sale were used to finance the Contras rebels in Nicaragua. Reagan denied such allegations in beginning but they were later proven true by commissions of investigators.

General Motors Streetcar conspiracy

In the 1940s, a lot of urban legends and stories were circulating among Americans about the efforts of General Motors to monopolize transportation systems. Years later a court in California indicted not only General Motors but 8 other companies and 7 individuals on counts of conspiracy.

Watergate Scandal

The Watergate scandal is an affair of political spying. It started on June 17th, 1972 after the arrest of burglars who broke into a building in Washington DC that housed the DNC. Journalists from the Washington Post link the burglars to President Nixon and his illegal financing of elections. In the beginning nobody trusted them and Nixon was reelected in November 1972. Two years later the truth is revealed and Nixon resigns.

James Wilkinson

General James Wilkinson was hailed as an American war hero during in 1812. A lot of people at the time accused him of working for the Spanish kingdom. Years after his death, his treason was discovered and he was condemned by all American Historians. Theodore Roosevelt once said "In all our history, there is no more despicable character."
