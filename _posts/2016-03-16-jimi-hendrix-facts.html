Johnny Hendrix

Hendrix name at birth was Johnny Allen Hendrix. His father later changed his name to James Marshall Hendrix.

The Ukulele was his first instrument

The first instrument that Hendrix played was a ukulele he found in the trash.

Wrote his own screenplay

Hendrix wrote a screenplay called Moon Dust where the main character represented traits from Jimi's own personality.

High School Dropout

Though Hendrix went on to become wildly famous and successful, he never even finished high school. He dropped out before he enlisted in the military.

Comic Books Lover

Hendrix was a huge fan of Spiderman and Batman but loved comic books in general.

Music was his religion

Hendrix classified the music he played as part of the “electric church.” According to him, he wanted his music to be part of a safe place that anyone could access and experience his music in a sort of religious way. He liked the idea of music bringing people together without judgments.

He discovered Randy California

In 1966 Hendrix discovered guitarist Randy Wolfe later known as Randy California. Randy joined Hendrix's band in the summer of 1966 and in 1967 founded the rock band Spirit with Mark Andes and Jay Ferguson.

Joined the Army to Avoid Jail

Police caught Hendrix driving stolen cars when he was 19. Authorities made him choose between enlisting in the military or going to prison.

Autodidact

Hendrix first learned to play single notes on the ukulele he found in the garbage. When he was 15 he bought his first acoustic guitar and taught himself by playing for long hours everyday. He never learned how to read music notes.
