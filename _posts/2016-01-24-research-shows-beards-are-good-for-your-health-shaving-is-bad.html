It seems like being a beardy weirdy is finally going out of fashion. People are slowly returning to their clean-shaven ways. However people need to reconsider shaving off their facial hair, because bristles bring a whole load of unexpected health benefits.

Researchers swabbed the faces of 408 males and compared the bacteria that was found on those with a beard and those that were clean shaven. You would expect that a beard would be full off horrible bugs, but it would seem those with clean cheeks faired worst in this test.

The study said:

‘Our study suggests that facial hair does not increase the overall risk of bacterial colonisation compared to clean-shaven control subjects. Clean-shaven control subjects exhibited higher rates of colonisation with certain bacterial species.’

Apparently those with clean shaven skin are three times more likely to be carrying harmful bacteria. I would have thought it would have been the other way around, but the study explains:

“When you shave, you cause microtrauma to the skin, resulting in abrasions, which could support bacterial colonisation.”

There’s also evidence that points toward beards actually being able to fight off infection. BBC Two’s Trust Me, I’m a Doctor have a theory that beards are able to create antibodies that destroy harmful bacteria.
